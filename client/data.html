Key Findings & Implications

✅ Churn correlates strongly with RFM

Kruskal-Wallis confirmed RFM features differ significantly between churned and retained customers. Validates using RFM as the primary feature group for churn modelling.

✅ Revenue is right-skewed (log-normal)

Shapiro-Wilk confirmed non-normality. Log-transformation was applied for CLV regression, yielding R² = 0.931 on the Ridge model.

✅ Fraud amounts are statistically distinct

Mann-Whitney U confirmed fraudulent transactions have significantly higher amounts and occur more outside business hours — validating Amount as the key fraud feature.

ℹ️ Gender does not predict category preference

Chi-squared (p = 0.18) — no significant gender–category association. Personalisation must rely on behaviour, not demographics alone.
