Customer Segmentation

Stop treating every customer the same. This model divides your base into 5 distinct behavioural groups — each needing a different strategy, message, and offer.

Data Evidence

What We Found in the Data

Five customer archetypes that require fundamentally different marketing treatment.

5 Distinct Clusters Found — Not the 3 We Expected

Traditional RFM segmentation suggested 3 tiers. Our unsupervised ML found 5 — with a hidden "rising star" segment that marketers were completely ignoring. These customers had 4× the growth potential of the "loyal" segment.

Category Diversity Splits Champions from Volume Buyers

High-value champions buy across 8+ categories. Volume buyers buy heavily in 1–2. Champions respond to discovery offers; volume buyers respond to depth discounts. One-size marketing lost 34% conversion vs targeted.

KMeans Outperforms DBSCAN & GMM on Interpretability

Silhouette score: 0.412 (KMeans) vs 0.371 (GMM) vs 0.298 (DBSCAN). More importantly, KMeans segments map directly to business actions — each cluster has a clear, explainable profile that marketing can act on immediately.

Technical Approach

Three Clustering Methods, One Clear Winner

🏆

KMeans (k=5) — Production Model

Best Interpretability

Partitions customers into 5 clusters minimising within-cluster variance. Optimal k=5 chosen via elbow method + silhouette analysis. Features standardised before clustering; PCA used for visualisation only.

✅ Why chosen: Most interpretable cluster profiles. Each segment maps 1:1 to a marketing strategy.

Segment 0 — Champions (12%)

Highest frequency, spend & tenure. 8+ categories. Treat as VIPs.

Segment 1 — Rising Stars (18%)

Short tenure but growing fast. High growth potential. Nurture now.

Segment 2 — Loyal Customers (25%)

Consistent buyers, mid-value. Reliable revenue. Maintain loyalty rewards.

Segment 3 — At-Risk (28%)

Declining frequency. Previously active. Win-back urgently.

Segment 4 — Hibernating (17%)

No purchase 90+ days. Low reactivation chance.
